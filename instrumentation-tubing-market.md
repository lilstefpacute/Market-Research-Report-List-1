<p><p>The instrumentation tubing market is divided into two types: seamless tubing and welded tubing. Seamless tubing is manufactured by piercing a solid billet to form a hollow tube, which offers superior strength and pressure resistance. It is commonly used in applications where high performance and reliability are crucial, such as in the oil and gas industry. Welded tubing, on the other hand, is made by joining pieces of metal together through welding. It is typically less expensive than seamless tubing and is often used in less demanding applications.</p></p>
