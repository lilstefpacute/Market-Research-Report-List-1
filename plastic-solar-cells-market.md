<p><p>Plastic solar cells can be classified into two main types: Type I and Type II. Type I plastic solar cells consist of a bulk-heterojunction structure, where the donor and acceptor materials are intimately mixed on a nanoscale level. On the other hand, Type II plastic solar cells have a bulk heterojunction interface, but feature separate donor and acceptor domains. These distinct market types offer different advantages and limitations in terms of efficiency, cost-effectiveness, and manufacturing processes, thus catering to different consumer needs and preferences.</p></p>
