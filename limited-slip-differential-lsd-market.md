<p><p>A Limited Slip Differential (LSD) is a type of differential that allows two wheels on the same axle to rotate at different speeds. It is designed to provide improved traction and handling by ensuring that power is distributed evenly between the two wheels, even when one wheel loses grip.</p><p>The market for limited slip differentials is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.7% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for high-performance vehicles is driving the adoption of limited slip differentials, as they improve the vehicle's cornering and overall stability. Additionally, the rising popularity of off-road and sports vehicles is also fueling the demand for LSDs, as they enhance traction and control on uneven surfaces.</p><p>Another trend in the limited slip differential market is the growing preference for electronic LSDs. These differentials use electronic controls to distribute power between wheels, allowing for greater precision and flexibility in varying driving conditions. They also offer improved fuel efficiency compared to mechanical LSDs.</p><p>The market is also witnessing technological advancements, such as the development of torque vectoring LSDs.
