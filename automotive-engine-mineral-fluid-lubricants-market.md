<p><p>The automotive engine mineral fluid lubricants market is divided into three types: paraffinic lubricants, naphthenic lubricants, and aromatic lubricants. Paraffinic lubricants are derived from crude oil and have high stability and viscosity index, making them suitable for a wide range of temperatures. Naphthenic lubricants are also produced from crude oil but offer superior solvency and better low-temperature properties. Finally, aromatic lubricants contain a high percentage of aromatic hydrocarbons and are known for their excellent heat dissipation and oxidation resistance properties.</p></p>
