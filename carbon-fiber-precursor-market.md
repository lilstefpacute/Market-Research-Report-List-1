<p><p>The Carbon Fiber Precursor Market is primarily categorized into three types: PAN-based, Coal Pitch-based, and Other. PAN-based precursors are derived from a polymer called polyacrylonitrile and are the most commonly used type due to their excellent mechanical properties. Coal Pitch-based precursors, as the name suggests, are obtained from coal tar pitch and offer enhanced thermal stability. The "Other" category includes precursors made from petroleum pitch, rayon, and cellulose, among others. Each type of precursor offers distinct characteristics and contributes to the diverse applications of carbon fiber in various industries.</p></p>
