<p><p>Drug-resistant tuberculosis (DR-TB) refers to strains of tuberculosis bacteria that are resistant to at least one of the primary anti-tuberculosis drugs. This form of tuberculosis is much more difficult to treat and poses a serious global health threat. The treatment for DR-TB involves a combination of second-line antibiotics, which are more expensive, more toxic, and have a longer duration of therapy compared to the standard first-line drugs used to treat drug-sensitive tuberculosis.</p><p>The current outlook of the DR-TB treatment market is challenging due to limited treatment options, higher costs, and longer treatment duration. However, several advancements in treatment strategies and the development of new drugs offer hope for the future. </p><p>Newer drugs, such as bedaquiline and delamanid, have been shown to be effective against DR-TB and have fewer side effects. The introduction of these drugs has significantly improved the treatment outcomes for patients with DR-TB. Additionally, shorter treatment regimens under clinical trials show promising results in effectively treating DR-TB in a shorter duration, which can improve patient adherence and reduce drug resistance.</p><p>Despite the challenges, the global DR-TB treatment market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11% during the forecasted period. This growth is primarily driven by increased investments in research and development, collaborations among key stakeholders, and increasing awareness about the burden of DR-TB. Furthermore, the global initiatives like End TB Strategy by the World Health Organization aim to eliminate tuberculosis, including DR-TB, by 2035, which further contributes to the positive outlook of the DR-TB treatment market.</p></p>
