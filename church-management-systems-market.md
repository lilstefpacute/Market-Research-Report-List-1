<p><p>Church management systems are software tools designed to assist churches in managing their administrative, financial, and communication tasks. There are two main types of church management systems: electric and manual. </p><p>Electric church management systems are computer-based software that automate various church operations, offering features like member database management, attendance tracking, event scheduling, and online giving platforms. These systems allow churches to efficiently handle their administrative tasks and improve communication and engagement with their members.</p><p>On the other hand, manual church management systems involve traditional paper-based methods for managing church operations. This may include physical attendance registers, handwritten financial records, and physical communication channels like bulletin boards and paper newsletters. While manual systems may still be used by smaller churches or those with limited resources, they are less efficient and time-consuming compared to electric systems.</p></p>
