<p><p>Biomaterials are materials that are used in medical applications to interact with biological systems. There are several types of biomaterials in the market. Metallic biomaterials, such as titanium or stainless steel, are known for their strength and durability. Ceramic biomaterials, like alumina or zirconia, offer excellent biocompatibility and resistance to wear. Polymeric biomaterials, such as polymethyl methacrylate or polyethylene, are lightweight and flexible. Natural biomaterials, like collagen or silk, are derived from natural sources and often mimics the properties of human tissues. Composites combine different materials to achieve desired properties, such as biodegradability or improved mechanical strength.</p></p>
