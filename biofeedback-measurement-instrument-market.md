<p><p>Biofeedback measurement instruments are devices used to monitor and measure physiological responses in the human body. The market can be categorized into four types: brainwave, muscle, sweat glands, and other. Brainwave instruments measure electrical activity in the brain, providing insights into mental states and emotional reactions. Muscle instruments analyze muscular activity, aiding in understanding muscle tension and relaxation. Sweat gland instruments monitor perspiration levels, helping assess stress and anxiety levels. Other instruments encompass a wide range of physiological measurements like heart rate, blood pressure, and skin temperature, providing comprehensive data for biofeedback therapy and research.</p></p>
