<p><p>A photosensitizer is a substance that absorbs light energy and then transfers it to a different molecule, triggering a chemical reaction. In the field of medicine, photosensitizers are used in photodynamic therapy (PDT), a non-invasive treatment for various diseases including cancer, bacterial infections, and dermatological conditions. During PDT, a photosensitizer is administered to the patient, which subsequently accumulates in the targeted cells. When the photosensitizer is irradiated with light of a specific wavelength, it undergoes a photochemical reaction that generates reactive oxygen species, leading to the destruction of the targeted cells.</p><p>The future outlook of the photosensitizer market appears promising. The rising incidence of cancer and the increasing preference for non-invasive and personalized treatment options are expected to drive the demand for photosensitizers. Additionally, advancements in imaging technologies, such as fluorescence imaging, have improved the detection and specificity of photosensitizers, further boosting their adoption in the medical field.</p><p>Furthermore, the market for photosensitizers is likely to benefit from ongoing research and development efforts to enhance the efficacy and safety of PDT.
