<p><p>Parkinson's disease drug market encompasses different types of medications, including Carbidopa/Levodopa, Dopamine Receptor Agonists, and MAO-Inhibitors. Carbidopa/Levodopa is the most common treatment, working by increasing dopamine levels in the brain. Dopamine Receptor Agonists mimic the effects of dopamine, stimulating dopamine receptors directly. MAO-Inhibitors inhibit an enzyme called monoamine oxidase, which breaks down dopamine. These drugs help control Parkinson's symptoms, including tremors and stiffness. Understanding these market types is crucial for healthcare professionals to select the most suitable medication for each patient's needs.</p></p>
