<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The Motorcycle Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) sensors market consists of two main types: Attitude and Heading Reference System (AHRS) and Inertial Navigation System (INS). </p><p>AHRS sensors are used to measure the orientation (attitude) and direction (heading) of the motorcycle. They integrate data from various sensors like accelerometers, gyroscopes, and magnetometers to provide accurate information about the bike's position and orientation.</p><p>On the other hand, INS sensors combine AHRS with additional data, such as GPS information, to enable precise navigation and location tracking for motorcycles. They use constant velocity and acceleration measurements to estimate the bike's position, velocity, and attitude, making them ideal for applications like vehicle tracking systems and autonomous navigation.</p></p>
