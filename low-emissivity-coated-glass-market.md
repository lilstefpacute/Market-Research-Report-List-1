<p><p>Low emissivity (low-e) coated glass is a type of glazing material that is designed to minimize the amount of heat transfer and UV radiation that passes through it. The glass is coated with a microscopically thin layer of metallic oxides, typically silver or tin oxide, which helps to reflect heat back into the room and reduce solar heat gain. This type of glass is commonly used in windows, doors, and other architectural applications.</p><p>The low emissivity coated glass market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
