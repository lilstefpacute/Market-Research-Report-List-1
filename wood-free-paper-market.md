<p><p>Wood-free paper, also known as acid-free paper or alkaline paper, is a type of paper that is manufactured using chemical pulp rather than mechanical pulp. The main advantage of wood-free paper is its longevity, as it is resistant to yellowing or deterioration over time.</p><p>The wood-free paper market has been witnessing steady growth in recent years. <p><p>The wood-free paper market consists of two major types: coated wood-free paper and uncoated wood-free paper. Coated wood-free paper is made by applying a coating, usually of clay or other minerals, onto one or both sides of the paper. This coating provides a smooth surface, enhances the brightness of the paper, and improves its printability. On the other hand, uncoated wood-free paper does not have any coating, resulting in a more natural and rougher texture. It is widely used for applications that do not require high print quality or sharp images.</p></p>
