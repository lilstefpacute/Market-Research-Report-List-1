<p><p>Microcontrollers (MCUs) are embedded systems designed for specific applications. The market for MCUs can be categorized into three types based on their data processing capacity: 8-bit, 16-bit, and 32-bit. An 8-bit microcontroller has a limited processing capacity, suitable for simple tasks such as remote control, small appliances, or basic sensors. The 16-bit microcontroller provides a better capability, enabling more complex tasks like motor control or advanced digital signal processing. Lastly, the 32-bit microcontroller offers higher processing power suitable for demanding applications like industrial automation, robotics, or advanced communication systems.</p></p>
