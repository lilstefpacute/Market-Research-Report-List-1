<p><p>The silk sewing thread market consists of various types such as Mulberry Silk, Tussar Silk, and Eri Silk. Tussar Silk, also known as wild silk, is produced by the Antheraea moth and has a slightly coarse texture. Eri Silk, sourced from the Philosamia ricini silkworm, offers a silk thread with a soft and warm feel. These different silk types cater to diverse consumer preferences and applications in the sewing industry.</p></p>
