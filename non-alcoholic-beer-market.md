<p><p>The non-alcoholic beer market consists of different types, including "Limit Fermentation" and "Dealcoholization Method." In the "Limit Fermentation" method, the beer is brewed using traditional ingredients like barley and hops, but its fermentation process is stopped before it can produce high alcohol content. On the other hand, the "Dealcoholization Method" involves removing alcohol from already brewed beer using various techniques, such as vacuum distillation or reverse osmosis. Both methods enable the production of non-alcoholic beer while maintaining flavor and aroma, catering to consumers who prefer alcohol-free alternatives.</p></p>
