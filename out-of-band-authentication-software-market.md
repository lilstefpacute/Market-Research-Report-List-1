<p><p>Out of Band Authentication (OOBA) software market consists of two main types of authentication methods: Two-Factor Certification and Multifactor Certification. Two-Factor Certification requires users to provide two separate pieces of evidence to verify their identity, such as a password and a security token. Multifactor Certification, on the other hand, involves the use of multiple authentication factors, which can include passwords, security tokens, biometrics, or other unique identifiers. Both types of certification aim to enhance security by adding an extra layer of authentication to ensure only authorized individuals can access sensitive information or systems.</p></p>
