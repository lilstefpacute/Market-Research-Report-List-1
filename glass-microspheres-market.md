<p><p>Glass microspheres are small, spherical particles made of glass that find applications in various industries. They are categorized into two types: hollow and solid. Hollow glass microspheres are empty inside, providing them with excellent insulation properties and low density. They are commonly used in lightweight filler applications, such as in the aerospace, automotive, and construction industries. On the other hand, solid glass microspheres have a solid core and are mainly used in applications like paint and coating formulations, where they act as fillers, reducing the overall weight and improving performance characteristics.</p></p>
