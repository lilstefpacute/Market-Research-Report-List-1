In the IDA Process, glyphosate is synthesized using a complex chemical reaction involving the condensation of iminodiacetic acid (IDA) and phosphorus trichloride. On the other hand, Glycine Process involves the reaction between glycine and formaldehyde to produce glyphosate. These two processes differ in terms of their raw materials and reaction mechanisms, but both result in the production of glyphosate for use as an effective herbicide in agriculture and weed control applications.</p></p>
