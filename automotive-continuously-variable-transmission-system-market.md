<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The automotive continuously variable transmission (CVT) system market comprises two main types: belt-chain drive CVT and planetary CVT. The belt-chain drive CVT employs a belt or chain mechanism that connects two variable diameter pulleys, allowing for infinite gear ratios and seamless shifting. On the other hand, the planetary CVT employs a set of gears that rotate around a central sun gear, providing power transmission with higher efficiency. Both types offer advantages such as improved fuel efficiency and smoother driving experience, catering to different customer preferences in the automotive industry.</p></p>
