<p><p>Recycled elastomers are materials that are derived from the recycling of rubber products such as tires, automotive parts, and industrial goods. The increasing awareness about the environmental impact of waste rubber disposal and the rising demand for sustainable materials are the key factors driving the market growth. Additionally, the growing automotive and tire manufacturing industries, especially in developing countries, are also contributing to the market expansion.</p><p>One of the major advantages of recycled elastomers is their cost-effectiveness compared to new rubber materials. The production of recycled elastomers requires less energy and resources, making them an attractive option for various industries. Moreover, these elastomers offer similar properties and performance as virgin rubber, making them suitable for a wide range of applications.</p><p>However, there are certain challenges that the recycled elastomers market may face. The lack of proper collection and recycling infrastructure in some regions may hinder the growth of the market.
